Overview: Understanding Philippine GDP

A visual exploration of how the Philippine economy has evolved across regions, provinces and industries ‚Äî and where it's heading.

Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is the total monetary value of all goods and services produced within a country‚Äôs borders over a specific time period. It is one of the most essential indicators used to gauge the health, productivity, and direction of an economy.

In the context of the Philippines ‚Äî a rapidly developing archipelagic nation with highly diverse regions ‚Äî GDP provides a crucial lens through which we can assess the country's economic balance. With over 7,600 islands and 17 regions, economic output varies widely across provinces and Highly Urbanized Cities (HUCs), reflecting differences in industrial specialization, urbanization, infrastructure, and resource availability.

This interactive dashboard visualizes the Philippine GDP from 2018 to 2023, broken down by Province, HUCs, and industry classifications such as Agriculture, Manufacturing, Services, and more. The analysis includes not only absolute GDP values but also growth rates and percentage share contributions at both regional and provincial levels.

A regionalized view of GDP helps:

  • Pinpoint key economic drivers in each region
  • Detect imbalances and emerging growth centers
  • Inform national and local government decisions on budgeting, infrastructure, and development planning
  • Empower researchers, investors, and the public with transparent economic data

In a country where Metro Manila (NCR) historically dominates economic output, while regions like BARMM, CARAGA, and MIMAROPA have often lagged behind, this tool serves as a vital step toward understanding ‚Äî and potentially closing ‚Äî these economic gaps.

GDP Per Capita (2023)

GDP per capita represents the average economic output per person in the Philippines. It is calculated by dividing the country's total GDP by its population.

For 2023, this value is approximately $3,950, reflecting a steady rise over recent years.

Source: World Bank

GDP Growth Rate (2023)

The Philippines recorded a 5.6% GDP growth in 2023. This figure represents the year-over-year increase in total economic output.

Despite global uncertainties, the Philippine economy showed resilience driven by strong domestic demand, infrastructure spending, and service sector recovery.

Source: World Bank

Total GDP (2023)

The Philippine economy produced goods and services worth approximately $435 billion in 2023, based on current USD exchange rates and nominal GDP estimates.

This total includes output from all industries: agriculture, manufacturing, construction, services, and more.

Source: World Bank

üîπ Model Summary

Goal: Explain forecasting methods and limitations.

Model Used: Facebook Prophet

The forecasts in this dashboard were generated using Facebook Prophet, a powerful open-source time-series forecasting library developed by Meta.

  • Works well with historical yearly GDP data
  • Captures trends and seasonality automatically
  • Handles missing values and outliers robustly
  • Widely used in business forecasting for its interpretability

Prophet models time-series as an additive model: y(t) = trend(t) + seasonality(t) + holidays(t) + error

Forecast Assumptions and Limitations

The forecasts are subject to several assumptions and limitations:

  • üìÖ Data assumes consistent trends and patterns will persist
  • üìâ Effects of inflation, policy shifts, or global crises are not explicitly modeled
  • üî¢ Some provinces or sectors have limited historical data, reducing forecast precision
  • üìà GDP values are forecasted purely from historical trends without macroeconomic predictors
  • üß† Human factors and unpredictable external shocks (e.g., pandemics, conflicts) are not included

Use these projections as trend indicators, not absolute predictions.
